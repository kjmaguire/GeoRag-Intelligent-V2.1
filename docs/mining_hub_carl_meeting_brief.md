## 5-minute opener

> "We're building GeoRAG — a geological intelligence platform for junior miners that ingests their internal exploration data plus public geoscience data, lets geologists ask natural-language questions with cited answers, and exports to QGIS, Leapfrog, and Python. Anna mentioned you, so I want to understand how Mining Hub works and where there might be overlap. Not a sales pitch — I'd rather come out of this knowing whether we should keep talking than waste your time on a demo."

---

## Questions, in priority order

1. **Channel.**
   How do juniors find Mining Hub today? What does an introduction to a junior look like — a recommendation, a featured listing, outbound sales?
   *Why I'm asking:* Tests whether co-marketing or referral makes sense.

2. **Audience.**
   Of the juniors on Mining Hub, roughly what fraction are in active exploration vs. development vs. M&A-shell mode?
   *Why I'm asking:* GeoRAG's ideal customer is an active explorer with a real historic-data pile. If Mining Hub's audience skews to shell companies, the channel doesn't help us.

3. **Press-release archive.**
   Is the corpus of press releases queryable? Is there an API or feed? Can it be licensed for ingestion into a customer's GeoRAG corpus?
   *Why I'm asking:* Press releases are good secondary evidence for project history — Mining Hub might be a data source even if it isn't a sales channel.

4. **Integration appetite.**
   Have you ever wired Mining Hub to a third-party tool? What did that look like?
   *Why I'm asking:* Tests integration option without committing to anything.

5. **Existing pain.**
   What do the juniors on Mining Hub complain about most when it comes to their internal data or technical workflow?
   *Why I'm asking:* Tests whether there's a wedge we don't know about yet.

---

## Closing ask

> "Can you introduce me to one junior on Mining Hub who's actively exploring and has a real data pile? I'd want to see if GeoRAG would actually help them, and a 30-minute call with them is the fastest way to find out."

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't pitch the product or the architecture.** This is a fit conversation. If Carl asks for a demo, schedule a separate call.
- **Don't talk through screen-shares unless asked.** Audio-only keeps the conversation about the relationship, not the software.
- **Don't promise a partnership or integration on this call.** Take notes, decide later.
- **Don't oversell First Nations consultation, prospectivity, or VL summarization.** Anchor on the things customers can already do today (ingest, query with citations, export to QGIS/Leapfrog).
