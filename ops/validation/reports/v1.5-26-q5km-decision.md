## Why Q5_K_M loses on this hardware

1. **VRAM overflow drives 70% throughput collapse.** Q5_K_M weights
   (~21 GB) overflow the A4500's 20 GB ceiling. Ollama spills ~1.5 GB
   of weights + extra KV-cache pressure to host RAM. Even with
   `QWEN3_NUM_THREAD=12` on the Threadripper, Zen 3 cores can't match
   the GPU's 640 GB/s memory bandwidth → tok/s collapses from 101 to 30.
2. **2507 Instruct fine-tune confound.** The available Q5_K_M GGUF is
   from `unsloth/Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507-GGUF` — that's a *different*
   base fine-tune (the 2507 instruct variant) than the Ollama default
   `qwen3:30b-a3b`. Output style is more terse (mean 1463 tokens vs
   2860 baseline). On the keyword rubric, terser answers hit fewer
   rubric keywords even when accurate.
3. **Keyword-rubric ceiling.** Q5_K_M scored 0.83 on Faults and Geochem
   where Q4_K_M scored 1.00, but the answer text was substantively
   correct — just shorter. An LLM-as-judge pass would likely narrow
   this gap, but the throughput hit is decisive regardless.

## Implication for capacity-planning

**The 20 GB A4500 is the binding constraint for Q5_K_M.** A pure
Q5_K_M-vs-Q4_K_M comparison at quality-equivalent throughput requires
≥24 GB VRAM where the model fits without spillover. On this hardware,
Q4_K_M is the right default and the v1.5-26 decision is settled.

## What this run actually proved

- The **A4500 + Threadripper combo runs Q4_K_M at ~100 tok/s warm** —
  6.4× the prior RTX 4080 baseline (14.6 tok/s). This is the headline
  capacity-planning datapoint.
- The **MoE telemetry sidecar correctly reports the Q4_K_M offload at
  ~825 MB / 4.2% of layers** during inference, and goes to 0 when the
  model unloads. Layer-1 metrics (VRAM split, KV pressure, scrape
  failures) are all live and accurate.
- The **validator path itself is solid** on this hardware — 5 prompts
  per model, ~25-90s each, deterministic via seed=42. Future quant
  comparisons can rerun with one env flip.
